The Problem

Advertising Technology Is Structurally Blind

Every major advertising platform — Google, Meta, Trade Desk, Amazon — predicts what you'll buy based on what you've already done. This approach has three fatal limits.

Limit 1 Can't Explain

Correlational systems know that a pattern exists but not why. When creative A outperforms creative B, they can tell you which won — but not what psychological mechanism drove the result, making it impossible to systematically reproduce success.

Limit 2 Can't Transfer

Insights are locked to the exact context where they were learned. A luxury travel campaign that spent $50K learning what works gives zero benefit to a premium hospitality campaign — even though the same buyer psychology drives both.

Limit 3 Can't Adapt

New products, new audiences, new market conditions = no historical data = the system goes blind. The entire optimization has to restart from scratch. Every cold start is expensive.

"Every other platform in this space is trying to predict behavior from behavior. We predict behavior from psychology — and the evidence comes from the largest bilateral psychological evidence base ever constructed."

The Data Foundation

937 Million Reviews.
Both Sides of Every Purchase.

INFORMATIV's core asset is a bilateral psychological evidence base — the only one in existence — that captures both the buyer's mind and the seller's strategy for every transaction.

Buyer Side What the Customer Was Thinking

From 937M+ product reviews across 32 product categories and 12 data sources, we extract 73 psychological constructs per review: decision-making style, risk orientation, social needs, emotional patterns, temporal horizon, cognitive processing depth.

This isn't sentiment analysis. It's a clinical-grade psychological profile derived from how people describe their purchase experience.

Seller Side What the Brand Was Doing

From 2,748 annotated product descriptions, we extract 65 psychological constructs: which persuasion strategies the creative uses, what aspirational framings are deployed, which authority signals are present, how urgency is manufactured.

Every product has a persuasion fingerprint — the specific psychological techniques its marketing deploys, whether the brand knows it or not.

The Match 6.75 Million Bilateral Evidence Edges

When a buyer's psychology aligns with a seller's strategy and a purchase occurs, we capture that alignment as a 27-dimension bilateral edge. 6.75 million verified purchase events, each scored on 27 psychological dimensions from both sides simultaneously.

This is analogous to a clinical trial dataset that records both the patient's condition and the treatment's mechanism of action for every outcome — not just "treatment worked" or "treatment didn't."

Breakthrough Nonconscious Goal Activation

The system doesn't just match ads to user demographics. It understands how page content activates nonconscious goals in the reader — before any ad is shown.

When someone reads an article about executive productivity, it doesn't just put them in a "business" topic category. It automatically activates goals around competence, status, and time efficiency. An ad for premium transportation fulfills those already-active goals — the person notices it because their attention system is primed for solutions.

This is grounded in 30 years of peer-reviewed research on automatic goal pursuit. The system profiles 8 foundational goals per page and matches ads to goal fulfillment — moving from "topic relevance" to "goal completion."

Architecture Knowledge Propagation — The System's Brain

In the human brain, learning something new doesn't stay local — it cascades through every connected neural pathway, strengthening some connections and weakening others. The entire network reorganizes around the new knowledge.

INFORMATIV works the same way. When a single conversion occurs, that observation doesn't just update one statistical model. It propagates through 14 connected subsystems via 16 transformation pathways — each one defining how knowledge changes as it crosses the boundary between subsystems:

Each propagation is damped (like neural signal decay) so the cascade doesn't oscillate. Some pathways fire immediately; others accumulate evidence before triggering. The result: every impression reorganizes the entire system's understanding — just like how a brain learns.

Core 5-Level Learning Hierarchy

Knowledge flows from specific to general and back:

  • Corpus level: What works across all brands and all categories
  • Category level: What's specific to luxury transportation
  • Brand level: What's specific to LUXY Ride
  • Campaign level: What's specific to this audience segment
  • Individual level: What's specific to this person

A new brand at Level 3 instantly inherits all Level 1-2 intelligence. It never starts from zero.

Multiplier Counterfactual Learning

When the system shows a social proof ad and it converts, conventional systems learn one thing: "social proof worked here." INFORMATIV also estimates what would have happened with authority, scarcity, or cognitive ease — learning about all possible approaches from a single outcome.

This multiplies the learning rate approximately 4x, meaning the system reaches optimization in a quarter of the impressions competitors require.

Protection Signal Quality Filtering

30-50% of ad impressions are never actually processed by the viewer — below the fold, scrolled past in under a second, or rendered on a background tab. Conventional systems learn from this noise equally, contaminating their models.

INFORMATIV's processing depth detector identifies and downweights unprocessed impressions, so the learning systems train on clean signal. This is the single highest-leverage quality improvement: cleaner data produces exponentially better models.

Adaptive Retargeting as Diagnosis

When someone doesn't convert, conventional retargeting shows the same ad again. INFORMATIV diagnoses the specific psychological barrier — trust deficit, processing overload, price sensitivity — and selects a targeted intervention to resolve it.

Each subsequent touchpoint is a prescription, not a repetition. The system tracks barrier resolution progress and adapts the intervention if the diagnosis changes.

Breakthrough Inferential Learning — Theory Building, Not Pattern Matching

This is the deepest differentiator. Conventional systems do parameter learning: "authority converts at 3.2%." INFORMATIV does theory building: it generates testable propositions about WHY things work, validates them against data, and revises them when wrong.

The system builds understanding at six levels of increasing complexity, each unlocked progressively as evidence accumulates:

LevelUnlocksWhat the System Understands
1 Week 1 "Authority is the most effective mechanism for the Careful Truster archetype"
2 Week 2 "...specifically on business and finance pages — 49% more effective than average"
3 Month 1 "...because those pages activate competence verification goals, and authority framing fulfills that goal — this is automatic goal-directed attention, not topic matching"
4 Month 2 "...but only when the user processes the ad for more than 2 seconds, because authority arguments require conscious evaluation to be persuasive"
5 Month 3 "...and the effect decays after 3 exposures as the buyer develops awareness of the persuasion tactic — the system should switch mechanisms at touch 3"
6 Month 4+ "The rate at which we're learning is accelerating — competitors haven't saturated this approach yet. Increase investment while the advantage lasts."

Each proposition is testable (makes specific predictions), falsifiable (can be disproved by data), grounded (in published psychology), and actionable (maps to a specific campaign change). The system runs a weekly scientific cycle: Observe → Validate → Hypothesize → Design Experiments → Apply.

"Machine learning finds patterns. We build theories. A pattern says 'this works.' A theory says 'this works because of A, under conditions B, except when C happens — and here's the experiment that will prove it.'"

When a conversion occurs, StackAdapt sends the event back to our server. If a user sees the ad but doesn't convert, the system doesn't just "retarget with the same ad." It diagnoses the specific psychological barrier — was it a trust deficit? Processing overload? Price sensitivity? — and selects a targeted intervention to resolve that specific barrier on the next touchpoint. Each subsequent impression is a prescription, not a repetition.
