Channel Openness Score
How receptive this audience is to advertising
Channel openness measures whether the reader is psychologically receptive to advertising right now. It combines cognitive engagement (are they paying attention?), arousal (are they activated?), approach orientation (are they open to new information?), and decision entropy (are they confused?). A score below 0.3 means the channel is "closed" — the reader is fatigued or hostile, and serving ads would waste budget. DSPs can use this to avoid bidding on low-receptivity impressions.

Page Intelligence: The Third Side of Trilateral Resonance
Every page on the web puts the reader in a specific psychological state before any ad loads. INFORMATIV pre-scores pages offline across 14 psychological layers, builds hierarchical domain taxonomies for prediction, and runs 12 daily intelligence strengthening tasks — all consumed at bid time in <2ms via Redis lookups. This is the page psychology layer that completes the trilateral resonance: buyer × ad × page.
1
Three-Pass Scoring Architecture
Progressive depth: NLP → DOM Analysis → Deep LLM
Each page is scored through up to three passes of increasing depth and cost. Pass 1 (NLP) runs on every discovered URL. Pass 2 (DOM) runs on high-value pages. Pass 3 (Deep LLM) runs on strategic pages where precision justifies cost. All results are cached in Redis with 24h TTL for instant bid-time lookup.
