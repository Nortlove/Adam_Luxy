Page Intelligence

Every page puts the reader in a specific psychological state before they see any ad. An article about inflation creates financial anxiety. A product review activates analytical evaluation. A holiday gift guide triggers social gifting urgency. INFORMATIV pre-scores these pages offline so at bid time, the system already knows the reader's psychological state — and can match the right ad to the right mind on the right page.

This is the trilateral resonance layer: buyer psychology × ad psychology × page psychology.

14 psychological layers, scored offline
Every page is analyzed across 14 intelligence layers — from activated needs to narrative arc to attention competition. All scored offline and cached in Redis for <2ms lookup at bid time.
Layers 1–4
What the reader needs & feels
Activated needs (security, belonging, status), emotional field (valence, arousal, trajectory), cognitive state (bandwidth, processing mode), credibility context (publisher authority, trust transfer).
Layers 5–8
How they'll respond to your ad
Primed categories (what products they're thinking about), persuasion channels (which mechanisms are OPEN vs CLOSED), competitive environment (ad density), decision-making style (ELM route, risk orientation, temporal frame).
Layers 9–11
Content structure intelligence
Narrative arc (tension→release, crescendo, steady), rhetorical structure (deductive, inductive, narrative — determines ELM route precisely), semantic embedding (vector similarity for brand×page matching).
Layers 12–14
Environmental context
Social proof signals (comments, shares, ratings), temporal context (breaking news vs evergreen, seasonal), attention competition (ad slots, CTAs, autoplay video, estimated viewability).
