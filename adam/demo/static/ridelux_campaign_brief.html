Campaign Track 1
Corporate Executive — "The executive choice"
Who they are: C-suite and senior managers who need reliable, status-appropriate ground transportation. They value efficiency over price, and their biggest fear is being late or arriving in a substandard vehicle.

Why commitment + authority works: These buyers have high brand loyalty once trust is earned (brand_relationship_depth: 0.65). They value consistency and reliability. They have low autonomy reactance (0.25) — they're receptive to being told "this is the right choice" by a credible source.

    Campaign Track 2
    Airport Anxiety Traveler — "Your ride is guaranteed"
    Who they are: Travelers within 24-72 hours of a flight who are actively worried about logistics. Their dominant emotion is anxiety — will the driver show up? Will there be hidden fees? Will I miss my flight?

    Critical Avoidance
    What will backfire — environments and tactics to avoid
    Don't Place On
    BuzzFeed / viral entertainment: Impulse-driven, novelty-seeking audience. Every psychological dimension misaligns with RideLux's reliability positioning.

    TikTok / short-form social: Attention spans too short, audience too young, content too casual. RideLux's professional voice feels foreign here.

    Deal/coupon sites: Price-first mindset. Seeing RideLux next to "50% off" coupons destroys the premium perception.
    Don't Say
    "Book now! Limited spots!" — Scarcity tactics feel manipulative to RideLux's careful, research-driven buyers. Their autonomy reactance will reject urgency pressure.

    "Cheapest luxury ride" — Price-leading destroys the premium frame. RideLux buyers pay for certainty, not savings.

    "You deserve to be pampered" — Too soft/indulgent for the corporate archetype. They want COMPETENCE, not pampering.
    Real-Time Intelligence
    What happens at bid time
    These campaign tracks define the STRATEGY. But within each track, every individual impression gets real-time optimization from INFORMATIV's decision engine:
    For every bid request StackAdapt sends us, the system:

    1. Reads the page title, referrer, and keywords to determine the reader's exact psychological state
    2. Checks which archetype best matches the available buyer signals
    3. Adjusts the mechanism if the page state suggests a different approach (e.g., a CNN Business article about trade war shifts the Corporate Executive from commitment → loss_aversion)
    4. Modifies creative parameters: if the reader arrived from Google search, increase concrete detail; if from social media, increase social proof
    5. Factors in environmental context: tax season boosts authority 1.4x; cultural events shift emotional resonance
    6. Returns the optimized recommendation in under 12 milliseconds

    Result: The same campaign track produces different creative intelligence for every impression based on the reader's psychological state at that exact moment.
