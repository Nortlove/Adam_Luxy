1. Executive Summary

What this document is and what makes it different from every other campaign brief.

This brief is built on 3,103 bilateral psychological edges — computed alignments between 65-dimension buyer profiles and 65-dimension seller profiles across 14 luxury transportation brands. It identifies 5 psychologically distinct buyer types through data-driven clustering on 31 identity-stable dimensions, then determines which specific seller positioning converts each type using inferential statistics.

11. Retargeting Strategy — Sequential Mechanism Deployment

Each retargeting touch addresses the specific bilateral alignment threshold that FAILED on the previous touch. This is NOT standard retargeting — each impression deploys a DIFFERENT mechanism.

Principle: Standard retargeting shows the same ad again. This system identifies WHY the buyer didn't convert (which alignment dimension was sub-threshold) and deploys a DIFFERENT mechanism on the next touch specifically targeting that failure. The sequence is statistically derived from non-converter sub-cluster analysis.

Careful Truster Retargeting

225 non-converters (35%) — High negativity_bias_match (fail=0.469 vs conv=0.169) means they weight negative information too heavily. Low brand_trust_fit (fail=0.319 vs conv=0.428) means the brand hasn't earned their trust yet. Low emotional_resonance (fail=0.358 vs conv=0.501) means the messaging felt transactional, not human.

Non-Converter Sub-Clusters

The Almost-Convinced (n=89): Good processing and appeal scores but low anchor_susceptibility. They evaluated but couldn't justify the price relative to alternatives.
Mechanism: price_anchor
Compare LUXY Ride price to hotel car service or Uber Black surge. 'JFK to Manhattan: Uber Black surge at 9 PM = $85-120. LUXY Ride = $65 guaranteed. Always.' Make the value comparison explicit.
The Fear-Frozen (n=25): Very high negativity_bias — they read the bad reviews and froze. Can't get past the fear of no-show/bad experience.
Mechanism: resolved_anxiety_narrative
Testimonial from someone who was ALSO skeptical: 'After 2 no-shows with [competitor], I was done with car services. My colleague insisted I try LUXY Ride. Driver was early. Car was clean. I've used them 11 times since.' The key: the testimonial author SHARES their psychology.
The Uninspired (n=111): Low mental_simulation and appeal resonance. The messaging didn't create a vivid picture of the experience. They couldn't imagine themselves using the service.
Mechanism: vivid_scenario
Specific, sensory-rich scene: 'Tuesday morning. 5:47 AM. Your LUXY Ride driver David is already at your building. Black Mercedes, engine running. Your name on the screen. Coffee-temperature water in the door. You get in, review your deck on the way to JFK. You arrive at Terminal 4 at 6:23 — exactly when you planned.'
