## Why an SSP, why Index Exchange, why now

### The post-cookie inflection has arrived

Cookie deprecation, ATT, GDPR signal loss — the substrate behavioral targeting depends on has eroded faster than industry alternatives have matured. Contextual targeting via topic classification is the obvious fallback, but that's category labels, not mechanism. Inferential systems beyond category labels are now the differentiator. The question isn't whether content-based-at-mechanism-depth wins; it's which inferential system becomes the standard.

### INFORMATIV is structurally privacy-native — and proven

Content × content × creative matching. No user IDs. No cookies. No third-party identity graphs. The LUXY pilot delivered `[X% lift]` without any of those, on the same creative as the lookalike control arm. The differentiator was audience selection at psychological depth.

### The cooperative publisher framing is foundational

Per ADAM's foundation: "publisher relationships are cooperative by design. Better matching makes their inventory more valuable. Privacy concerns that apply to behavioral scraping do not apply here, because the unit of analysis is the page's content, not the user's history." We are NOT a scraper-of-publisher-data play. The pilot results show CPM lift on bilateral-aligned inventory; the cooperative incentive aligns with the SSP economic interest.

### Why Index Exchange specifically

Index Exchange's reputation for transparent auctions + technical sophistication + premium inventory positions it for the kind of partnership that requires both sides to actually understand what's being matched. We are not asking IX to take our claims on faith — the case study, the conformal CI, the third-party-reviewed methodology, and the production system all exist. We are asking IX to integrate at a depth that lets publishers and DSPs both VERIFY the match quality on IX's own inventory.

## Concrete partnership shapes (three options to surface)

### Option A — Bid-time enrichment (lightest integration)

INFORMATIV provides an API: given a page URL + creative + bid context, returns a psychological-fit score + construct-chain explanation. IX adds the score as a key-value pair in the bid request. DSPs that consume the score bid higher on aligned impressions.

**Lift hypothesis on IX inventory**: bilateral-aligned impressions show measurably higher conversion rate at the same impression cost. Based on LUXY pilot: `[X% lift]`. We expect IX inventory in the same vertical to show comparable lift; cross-vertical will require local calibration but the substrate handles that.

**Lightest lift**: API callout, `[~50ms]` p95 latency budget, fallback behavior if INFORMATIV unavailable. Standard partner-API integration pattern.

### Option B — Annotated inventory tier (medium integration)

INFORMATIV batch-annotates IX inventory at the URL level on a daily/weekly cadence. Annotated URLs become a distinct **curated tier** advertisers / DSPs can opt into via PMP deals or open auctions tagged with the annotation.

**Differentiator**: Index Exchange's curated tier becomes "URLs annotated by INFORMATIV at psychological depth." Premium CPMs on the annotated tier.

**Publisher value**: improved yield on the annotated tier; full visibility into the annotation (publishers see what their content's profile is and can improve it strategically). The pilot validated this in the LUXY context: pages that scored higher on alignment-relevant dimensions cleared at materially higher CPMs in their auctions.

### Option C — Header-bidding key-value pair (deepest integration)

INFORMATIV's profile becomes a first-class signal in the header-bidding flow. Every bid request from IX-onboarded publishers includes the INFORMATIV cognitive profile as standardized key-value pairs. DSPs query against these in their bidding logic.

**Heaviest lift**: requires header-bidding standard adjustment, publisher SDK update, DSP-side decoder.

**Highest moat**: once INFORMATIV is in the header-bidding standard, it's structural to the IX value proposition.

### What we'd recommend (post-pilot framing)

Start with **Option A on a 30-day proof-of-replication** on a subset of IX inventory in a vertical adjacent to LUXY's (corporate travel, business services, premium hospitality). The 30-day timeline is shorter than a typical pilot because the substrate is proven; we're testing replication on IX inventory rather than validating the technology. If lift replicates on IX inventory at expected magnitudes, graduate to **Option B (annotated inventory tier)** in the following quarter. **Option C** is the 12-month conversation.

### "How is this different from existing contextual + AI approaches?"

Most contextual is **topic classification**. ADAM-level is **mechanism activation**. The LUXY pilot's per-archetype heterogeneity result was specifically the kind of finding mechanism-level can produce and topic-level cannot — different archetypes responded to different mechanisms on the same page-context, and the pilot recovered the differential lift with conformal coverage. Topic classification would have shown one number for the whole page; mechanism-level showed structure within the page that drove the result.

LLM-based contextual is statistical-correlation-flavored. INFORMATIV is mechanism-grounded — constructs come from peer-reviewed research, chains link causally, system embodies the mechanism rather than approximating it. This is the moat: you can't bolt mechanism onto a correlational system; the substrate is incompatible.
