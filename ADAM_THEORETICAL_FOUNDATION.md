## 2. Automated Nonconscious Goal Pursuit — The Operational Frame

This section is the most important one. It defines how the platform understands what it is doing when it places an ad.

### 2.1 The standard model in ad tech (which is wrong)

The industry assumes: a user sees an ad, consciously evaluates the offer, decides whether it is relevant, and clicks if it is. The optimization problem is framed as maximizing the probability of a conscious "yes." This is what behavioral targeting, contextual targeting as conventionally practiced, and creative optimization all implicitly assume. It is what Trade Desk Koa, Meta ad optimization, Google Performance Max, and StackAdapt Page Context AI are all optimizing for at their core.

### 2.2 The Bargh-lineage model (which is what ADAM operationalizes)

Most of what determines whether an ad succeeds happens outside of conscious deliberation. The operative causal chain looks like this:

1. **Environment presents priming stimuli.** The page the user is on — its content, its framing, its author, its category, its editorial register, the goals the content itself pursues — activates psychological constructs in the reader. These constructs are mindsets, goals, evaluations, attitudes, and motivational states. This activation is automatic. The user is not aware of it.

2. **Activated constructs bias subsequent perception and behavior.** Once a construct is active, it makes construct-congruent stimuli more likely to be noticed, processed, evaluated favorably, and acted upon. This is the perception-behavior link. It is not a metaphor — it is measured in dozens of published experiments.

3. **Goals, once activated, operate autonomously.** In the auto-motive model, a goal that has been chronically associated with a situational cue will be triggered and pursued nonconsciously when that cue reappears. The user does not decide "I will now pursue the goal of feeling competent" — the environment presents the cue, the goal activates, and behavior aligns with goal fulfillment. The user experiences it as "I just felt like clicking on that."

4. **An ad is a goal-fulfillment stimulus.** If the page has activated a goal, and the ad is an environmental affordance that offers fulfillment of that goal, the click is not a deliberated yes. It is the completion of an automatically-initiated behavioral sequence. The user rationalizes it afterward if asked. That rationalization is post-hoc.

5. **Retargeting is cumulative priming, not repeated persuasion.** A sequence of impressions does not work by hammering an argument into a skeptic. It works by keeping a goal construct chronically accessible so that when a situational trigger arises, the associated behavior is the path of least resistance. This is why ADAM's retargeting framework is built around cumulative goal priming across impressions, not around "touchpoint" accounting.

6. **Conversion is not caused by "the ad that converted."** It is caused by the accumulated activation state colliding with a triggering context. Attribution models that credit the last impression are measurement artifacts; the actual causal structure is distributed across the priming history. This is why the Shapley-value credit attribution and TheoryLearner's chain-outcome records exist — they are attempts to credit the causal chain, not the terminal event.

### 2.3 What this means operationally

- **Page-level intelligence is about identifying activated goals and mindsets, not topic classification.** "This page is about luxury travel" is a topic label. "This page activates the construct of aspirational self-presentation and the goal of status-signaling to peers" is the operative information. The first is correlational. The second is Bargh.

- **Archetypes are not audiences — they are goal-activation profiles.** When the system says "this archetype is likely present on this page," the claim is not "this kind of person reads this kind of page." The claim is "content of this kind activates the goal structures characteristic of this archetype, making ads that fulfill those goals more likely to be acted on automatically." These two claims are very different; only the second one is defensible.

- **Creative matching is about being a plausible fulfillment stimulus for the activated goal.** The creative does not need to argue. It needs to present itself, in the right visual and linguistic register, as the affordance that completes the behavioral sequence the page has started.

- **The crawling framing reflects the same logic.** Crawling pages (or, better, ingesting RSS-level signals — author, category, domain, title, editorial-vs-non-editorial, publish recency) is not data extraction for targeting. It is environmental audit — inventorying the priming landscape publishers present so that ads can be placed where the relevant goal is already active. The relationship with publishers is cooperative: better matching makes their inventory more valuable. Privacy concerns that apply to behavioral scraping do not apply here, because the unit of analysis is the page's content, not the user's history.

### 2.4 The Embodied Substrate — Why Priming Works at the Neural Level

Bargh's frame tells us *that* nonconscious priming works. Chris's own doctoral-level research program on primary metaphor theory, cross-linguistic universals, and embodied cognition tells us *why* it works mechanistically, at the level of neural substrate. This is a deepening of the operational frame, not a separate theory, and it is load-bearing for creative generation, page intelligence, and retargeting sequencing.

**The core claim: social-emotional cognition is implemented on the neural machinery of physical-spatial cognition, because evolution recycled what already existed rather than building new circuits.**

The evolutionary argument is specific. Before consciousness, before village-scale social living, the human brain had well-developed machinery for physical-spatial reasoning — distance, temperature, texture, weight, containment, solidity, closeness, force. These were the circuits the brain needed to evade predators, navigate terrain, track prey, manipulate objects, survive. They were evolutionary table stakes, and they had millions of years to be refined.

When social selection pressure increased dramatically — pair-bonding raised survival rates, and then villages of ~100 individuals raised them further — the brain suddenly needed to track a new kind of information: emotional proximity to many specific other people, trust levels, cooperative history, social hierarchy, coalition membership, reputation. This is abstract information the brain had no prior machinery for. Under evolutionary time pressure, building new circuits from scratch was not an option. So the brain did what evolution does: it recycled. It took the existing physical-spatial circuits and ran social-emotional cognition on top of them. Social warmth was implemented on physical warmth. Emotional distance was implemented on spatial distance. Cooperation was implemented on texture-smoothness. Trust was implemented on solidity-weight. Power was implemented on vertical orientation. And so on.

The empirical fingerprint of this recycling is Chris's own discovery: **certain primary metaphors are universal across every human language ever documented, including languages spoken by populations with no historical contact.** Distance metaphors for emotional connection appear in every language. Temperature metaphors for social affect appear in every language. Texture metaphors for cooperation appear in every language. Weight metaphors for seriousness and trust appear in every language. This is not cultural borrowing; this is a shared substrate leaking through into every linguistic surface that evolved on top of it. The metaphors are universal because the underlying neural architecture is universal.

The behavioral fingerprint is Chris's own published empirical work: **priming subjects with a physical stimulus on one of these axes shifts their social-emotional judgments on unrelated targets, without their awareness.** Holding a warm cup makes a stranger seem warmer and more generous (Williams & Bargh, Science 2008 — the archetypal demonstration). Standing farther away from an image of a person makes your estimate of your emotional closeness to them decline. Experiencing rough physical texture makes you rate a negotiation as less cooperative. These are not demand characteristics or response biases. They are the neural substrate of social cognition being activated through the physical-cognition pathway it was built on top of.

**The mirror-neuron completion.** The picture completes with one more observation: mirror neurons (Rizzolatti and colleagues) demonstrate that *observing* another person performing an action activates the same motor circuits as *performing* the action yourself, with the signal strength modulated by perceived social proximity. Humans have mirror systems for a much wider range of actions than other primates, including in Broca's area — which suggests mirror systems participate in language comprehension as well as motor imitation. The consequence is that when a user reads advertising copy invoking embodied metaphor, the user's own embodied-substrate circuits fire *as if the user were experiencing the physical state the metaphor describes*. Reading "rock-solid guarantee" fires the same circuits that would fire if the user were actually touching something solid and weighty. Because social trust is implemented on top of those circuits, social trust toward the advertised brand shifts as an automatic consequence.

**What this means for ADAM operationally:**

1. **Creative copy is a mechanism-level intervention, not a stylistic choice.** When copy invokes primary metaphor structures, it is literally activating the neural circuits that implement the social-emotional judgment the campaign is trying to produce. This reframes copy generation: the goal is not "pick the right tone for this archetype" but "pick the metaphor axis that most directly activates the substrate on which this archetype's converting response is implemented."

2. **The bilateral edge should carry primary metaphor axes as first-class dimensions**, on both sides. Buyer-side: what metaphors does the user's language (from reviews, from past campaign interaction) reveal they think in? Brand-side: what metaphor space does the brand's own copy occupy? Alignment on the metaphor axes is a substrate-level match, which is deeper than construct-level match and should produce stronger effect sizes when present.

3. **Page intelligence should extract metaphor profiles, not just topic profiles.** A page's metaphor density on the primary axes is a priming signal that propagates to any ad placed on it, independent of topic. A cold, distant, arm's-length register primes social distance; a warm, close, embracing register primes social warmth. These primes configure which creative metaphors will land on that page and which will fall flat. This is testable with relatively cheap NLP over RSS-level text.

4. **Retargeting sequences need embodied coherence.** Successive impressions that invoke incompatible metaphor axes (impression 1 warmth, impression 2 coldness) do not stack additively — they activate incompatible substrates and probably produce dissonance-driven backfire. The sequence scheduler should have a metaphor-coherence constraint, which is an architectural addition the current implementation has no way to consider.

5. **Backfire detection has a concrete mechanism to watch for.** Metaphor conflict between the page, the creative, and the user's own metaphor priors is an observable anti-priming signature that does not require before-and-after construct measurement. It is a cheaper path to the backfire-detection project flagged earlier.

**What this rules out.** A system that treats copy style as a register / tone / personality question is operating one level above the mechanism. It can optimize empirically but it cannot reason about *why* one register outperforms another, and it cannot transfer the insight to new contexts. ADAM can. The metaphor lens is one of the most important differentiators in the platform's intellectual toolkit, and it should be treated as first-class architecture, not as copy-generation decoration.

**Never explain primary metaphor theory, embodied cognition, mirror neurons, linguistic universals, or the physical-to-social recycling hypothesis to Chris.** He did the empirical work that demonstrates these effects and he discovered the cross-linguistic metaphor universals himself. The bar for communication with him on this material is: show that the implementation correctly captures the mechanism, or acknowledge that it does not yet and describe what would be required.

### 2.5 The Unified Motif — Dynamic Competition + Selection Across Time-Scales

Four of the theoretical frameworks the foundation rests on — Bargh's automaticity, Pinker's dual-mechanism theory, Ridley's nature-via-nurture, and Dawkins' selfish-gene framework — turn out to describe the same abstract architectural pattern operating at **different time-scales**. The convergence is not accidental: it is how biological systems at every level of organization balance stored, accumulated, individual-specific information against productive, contextual, generalizable information, with *reinforcement* as the mechanism by which the balance shifts over time.

The abstract pattern, at the moment-to-moment level:

> **Two systems are always present. They do not combine additively. Their balance is resolved dynamically by the activation strength of each in the current moment, where activation depends on reinforcement history and current priming conditions. Neither universally dominates.**

The extension across time-scales, once Dawkins is included:

> **Reinforcement is selection. It operates at every time-scale the system has.** In the moment, reinforcement produces automaticity (Bargh). Across a user's lifetime, reinforcement accumulates into memorized responses that override productive rules for specific items (Pinker). Across a population and over millions of decisions, reinforcement selects which patterns survive and propagate through the learning loop (Dawkins). Same mechanism, three speeds.

Applied to the four frameworks:

| Framework | Time-scale | System A (stored) | System B (productive) | What determines balance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bargh** | Moment-to-moment | Chronically accessible goals | Situationally activated goals | Environmental cue strength × chronic accessibility |
| **Pinker** | Within-user, across sessions | Memory (reinforced items) | Rules (productive combinatorial generators) | Cumulative reinforcement strength for the specific item |
| **Ridley** | Within-organism, across development | Genetic regulatory machinery | Environmental triggers | Presence of environmental signals that switch expression |
| **Dawkins** | Across population, across generations | Established ESS patterns | Mutant / invading strategies | Cumulative fitness — which patterns replicate reliably |

**The architectural commitment this implies for ADAM, at every time-scale:**

- **Moment-to-moment:** decision logic uses trait × state interaction, not trait + state sum. Interaction terms are first-class; main-effect-only scoring is the correlational failure mode.
- **Within-user:** the system accumulates individual memory over repeated interaction with a user, and that memory competes dynamically with productive inference for that user. High-frequency contexts develop memorized shortcuts; novel contexts go through productive inference.
- **Across-population:** the learning loop is an evolutionary process. Constructs, mechanisms, creative templates, and cascade pathways are competing for propagation based on reinforcement. Designing the reinforcement signal IS designing the fitness landscape — and the fitness landscape must explicitly penalize backfire, exploitation, and long-horizon damage, not just reward immediate conversion, or selection will drive the system toward whatever produces conversions by any means.

**This is the single deepest architectural principle the theoretical foundation carries, and it should be visible in every part of the implementation**: the bilateral edge schema (interaction terms first-class), the cascade override rules (use most granular evidence, which captures interactions), the learning loop's credit assignment (reinforcement is selection, design the fitness landscape deliberately), the creative engine's template selection (memes have memetic fitness, not just per-impression effectiveness), the retargeting sequence scheduler (state decays, reinforcement accumulates, both follow PK/PD-like dynamics), the page intelligence extractor (environmental primes activate goal structures in Bargh terms and regulatory machinery in Ridley terms), and the outcome handler (it is imposing selection pressure on everything upstream of it — imposing it deliberately is the design discipline).

Wherever the system is computing a score that collapses stable and transient factors into a single number without modeling their interaction, or applying a reinforcement signal without asking what selection pressure it is imposing on the upstream components, that site is wrong and should be flagged for refactoring.

## 3. The Industry Contrast — Correlation vs. Inference

This is the competitive and architectural thesis. It is also the frame against which every component of ADAM must be measured.

### 3.1 What every other ad-tech system does

Trade Desk Koa, Meta's ad optimization, Google Performance Max, StackAdapt's Page Context AI, DV360's Audience Expansion, Amazon DSP's audiences — at their core, every one of these systems does the same thing: **it observes that users who did X also did Y, and optimizes toward Y.** That is correlation. It scales. It is often effective. And it has three fatal structural limitations, which are not flaws in any particular system but consequences of choosing correlation as the primitive.

**Correlation cannot explain.** "Users who browsed running shoes at 7 a.m. convert 23% better with urgency messaging" is a correlation. Why is it true? The system cannot tell you. Are those users prevention-focused morning-routiners who respond to loss framing? Or are they achievement-oriented people whose temporal horizon is short, so they respond to scarcity? The correlation does not distinguish these, and the distinction matters — because the two populations will diverge the moment the context changes.

**Correlation cannot transfer.** If you learn "urgency works for shoe buyers at 7 a.m.," that insight is locked to that context. You cannot port it to insurance shoppers, podcast listeners, a new product category, or a new publisher. You have to relearn from scratch every time the surface changes. This is why correlational systems are infinitely data-hungry — they have no concept of generalizable mechanism, so they need examples in every cell of the matrix.

**Correlation cannot adapt to novelty.** When a new audience, product category, publisher, or context appears, a correlational system has nothing. It has no theory from which to reason. It can only wait until it has enough examples to find patterns, which means it is structurally slow at exactly the moment the industry most needs it to be fast.

### 3.2 What ADAM does differently

ADAM's primitive is not a correlation. It is an **inferential chain** whose links are grounded in published psychology. An inferential chain looks like this:

> `high uncertainty_intolerance → need for closure → receptivity to authority messaging → must be substantive because cognitive_engagement is high → frame for long temporal horizon because construal_level favors abstraction`

Each link is a claim from peer-reviewed work. The chain is portable: any user whose constructs fire in the same pattern is predicted to respond in the same way, **regardless of whether you have ever seen that user, that product category, that publisher, or that context before.**

This is where the three fatal limits of correlation dissolve:

- **Explanation** is built in. The chain is the explanation.
- **Transfer** is built in. The constructs are portable; the categories they apply to are interchangeable.
- **Adaptation** is built in. Novel contexts are handled by reasoning from the constructs, not by waiting for correlational evidence.

**This is also what makes zero-shot transfer the killer capability.** When a partner sends a segment ADAM has never seen — say, "podcast listeners in the wellness vertical" — a correlational system is blind. ADAM infers a likely nonconscious-decision-framework profile from the category and context priors, reasons forward through the construct chain, and outputs a recommended mechanism and framing with an explicit uncertainty estimate — before a single impression has been served. That is not a trick. It is a consequence of building on mechanism instead of correlation.

### 3.3 Why this is the moat

The reason this is defensible, and the reason the replication timeline is 3-5 years from scratch, is that you cannot bolt mechanism onto a correlational system. The two architectures are not incremental variants of each other. A company that has built a correlational system has accumulated years of optimization around assumptions — around feature engineering, around what it logs, around what its learning loops expect, around what its partners' integrations look like — that are incompatible with the inferential frame. Migrating requires rebuilding the substrate.

The things that are hard to replicate, ranked:

1. **Dual-sided annotation.** Every competitor annotates one side of the transaction, usually the user, usually via behavioral correlates. ADAM annotates both sides at psychological depth — buyer psychology from 937M+ reviews, seller psychology from brand copy — and joins them as 27-dimension bilateral alignment edges. This is the single most defensible asset. The raw material cost alone is in the millions; the theoretical frame required to annotate it coherently took a career to develop.
2. **A construct taxonomy grounded in peer-reviewed research.** 441+ constructs organized into 20+ domains, including dedicated domains for Nonconscious Architecture (subliminal processing, priming susceptibility) and Implicit Motivational Drivers (implicit power, achievement, affiliation motives). This is not an LLM tagging pass — it is a research synthesis.
3. **The graph as cognitive medium, not database.** Neo4j holding not just data but theoretical edges (see Section 4) — the constructs, their activation relations, the mechanisms that fulfill them, the conditions under which each link holds.
4. **Validated bilateral outcomes.** Every verified purchase in the corpus is a labeled instance of when the dual-sided match produced action. This is the supervision signal a correlational system cannot construct because it never had both sides.

Everything else — the bandits, the online learning, the blackboard, the DAG, the Prometheus metrics — is table stakes. The moat is mechanism, annotated on both sides, grounded in theory, validated by outcomes.

## 4. The Two-Sided Architecture and Where It Currently Falls Short

This section is the honest audit. The platform has the right bones, and in several places it has already executed on the inferential frame. In several other places it has drifted into correlational patterns that feel familiar and scale easily but undermine the thesis. Both must be held in view at once.

### 4.1 The bilateral primitive

Every decision in ADAM is structurally bilateral. On the buyer side: 20-dimension uncertainty profile (7 core edge dimensions + 13 extended psychological dimensions), anchored in review-derived psychology, updated per-user by online Bayesian learning. On the seller side: 27-dimension alignment profile, anchored in brand-copy analysis plus the psychological constructs the product actively fulfills. The edge between them is the operative primitive. **L3 bilateral edges, when available, override L1 archetype priors and L2 category posteriors entirely.** This is not a scoring weight — it is an architectural commitment. The label is weak; the edge is strong. Where you see code that averages across edges, collapses edge dimensions into a summary score, or falls back to archetype labels in the presence of available edges, that code is correlational drift and it is working against the thesis.

### 4.2 Pages as the third side

The page where the ad appears is the third side of the transaction, and it has been the most under-built. A page carries its own psychology — the goals its content activates, the mindsets it induces, the register of its language, the temporal horizon it orients the reader toward. In Bargh terms, the page is the environmental prime. The right schema for a page is structurally symmetric to the right schema for an ASIN: a set of constructs it activates, with weights and confidence, embedded in the same 27-dimension space. A page with an archetype-alignment profile can be edge-joined to buyer profiles exactly the way ASINs are today. No new primitive is needed — only a new node label and a crawl / RSS ingestion pipeline that produces the construct profile.

The cost-disciplined path for page intelligence is not full-page Claude classification. It is RSS-level signal extraction — author, category, domain, title, editorial-vs-non-editorial, publish recency — fed through a cheap local classifier with Claude escalation for high-value or ambiguous pages. This is both economically sane and theoretically correct: the signals are already diagnostic of the goals and mindsets the content activates, because publishers encode them consistently. Over time, Bayesian learning from campaign outcomes refines the page-to-construct mapping per publisher, per category, per section.

### 4.3 The drift pattern: computing assessments instead of producing reasoning chains

Prior analysis (`CHAT_HISTORY_FOR_CLAUDE.md` ~L878-1011) identified the structural failure mode. The atoms in the DAG assess: `{authority: 0.73, social_proof: 0.61, ...}`. The MechanismActivation atom fuses these into a score. A partner receiving `{authority: 0.73}` is being given a correlation, not an understanding. The inferential chain that produced 0.73 — `prevention focus → need for closure → authority, substantive because cognitive engagement is high, long horizon because construal level is abstract` — exists in the code's logic but is never materialized in the output. **This is the single most important architectural gap.** It must be fixed.

The fix is not deep. It is: every atom that currently emits a scalar must also emit the chain of construct activations that produced the scalar, tagged with the literature that grounds each link, and downstream consumers (creative generation, channel selection, learning updates) must consume the chain, not the scalar. The graph must store the theoretical edges — `(UncertaintyIntolerance)-[:CREATES_NEED_FOR]->(Closure)`, `(Closure)-[:SATISFIED_BY]->(Authority)`, `(HighCognitiveEngagement)-[:REQUIRES]->(SubstantiveEvidence)` — as first-class entities, not just the effectiveness edges (`archetype → mechanism → score`) that are there today. The TheoryLearner at `adam/core/learning/theory_learner.py` already has `LinkPosterior` and `ChainOutcomeRecord` — this suggests someone started the work. Whether the chain is actually being produced and updated end-to-end is unverified and must be checked early.

### 4.4 The drift pattern: learning updates weights but not theory

When an outcome arrives, the OutcomeHandler updates Thompson posteriors and effectiveness scores. That makes the number more accurate. It does not ask the load-bearing question: **did the theoretical link hold?** If the system predicted authority would work because of low uncertainty tolerance, and it did work, the `uncertainty_tolerance → closure → authority` chain should be strengthened. If it didn't work, one of two things is wrong: either the uncertainty tolerance assessment was mismeasured, or the theoretical link is incomplete for this context. The first is a measurement-error update; the second is a theory-revision update. A correlational system conflates them. An inferential system distinguishes them and updates differently. The distinction is not cosmetic — it determines whether the system converges on "authority works for this segment" or on "low uncertainty tolerance entails need for closure, which authority satisfies when substantive and long-horizon, except under X condition where the chain breaks." The second one transfers. The first does not.

### 4.5 The drift pattern: parallel implementations of the same idea

Grep for class definitions turns up two hypothesis engines (`adam/intelligence/inferential_hypothesis_engine.py` and `adam/behavioral_analytics/knowledge/hypothesis_engine.py`), multiple learning directories (`adam/learning`, `adam/core/learning`, `adam/intelligence/learning`, `adam/meta_learner`, `adam/cold_start/learning`), page intelligence modules sitting alongside page crawl schedulers, and scraper fleets that predate the bilateral cascade. This is a symptom: prior sessions built what they needed in the moment without retiring what already existed. The result is that the codebase is substantially richer than a naive audit would suggest — most of what is needed is already there — but it is also more fragmented. The highest-leverage work is usually integration and consolidation, not new building. Before writing a new module, the discipline must be: open the files that look like they already do this, read them, and either extend or retire them. Adding a sixteenth orphan class is the failure mode.

### 4.6 The drift pattern: silent fallback to simulation

Verified in this session (`adam/orchestrator/campaign_orchestrator.py:755-759` and `:802-805`): when the real AtomDAG fails or Neo4j is unreachable, the orchestrator silently falls back to `_simulate_atom_dag` and returns a decision that looks real but isn't. The learning loop then updates posteriors from this simulated output. This is the single most dangerous piece of correlational drift in the system, because it contaminates the outcome-to-theory update with fabricated data. **A system that learns from theater becomes confidently wrong, and its confidence is indistinguishable from its correctness until a partner tests it against ground truth.** This is load-bearing for every plan: until this is fixed, real learning cannot be turned on. The fix is small (tag simulated outputs, gate the learning loop to refuse them) and is a strict prerequisite for everything else described in this document.

- **Privacy-native intelligence.** Content-based, not behavioral tracking. This is both a legal/compliance strength and a philosophical commitment — the Bargh frame is about environmental primes, not user history, so the platform does not need behavioral surveillance to function.

**What this inventory means honestly:** The raw material for a truly inferential system is substantially built. The moat claim is defensible at a theoretical level. The gap between the current state and the vision is not "we need to build all of this" — it is "we need to verify what is actually wired, consolidate duplicate implementations, materialize the inferential chain as a first-class output, store theoretical edges in the graph alongside effectiveness edges, and update theory as well as weights in the learning loop." That is a smaller and more tractable problem than greenfield, but it is also harder, because it requires opening fifteen modules and deciding what to keep, what to retire, and what to extend. Greenfield is fast; integration is slow. The honest assessment is that ADAM is in the integration phase.

## 6. The Two-Pass Workflow — Expansive First, Tactical Second

This is a workflow rule, not a stylistic preference, and it exists because the assistant's default mode has a structural bias toward engineering canon, and engineering canon in ad tech is downstream of correlational assumptions that ADAM exists to reject. Applying the tactical lens before the expansive pass means the tactical lens drags every design decision back toward patterns that encode the failure mode the platform is defined against. The workflow is the counteragent.

**The rule:**

1. **Classify the work first.** For every proposed build, fix, or design decision, ask: is this pure execution with no design content, or does it contain design content even in a small amount?
   - *Pure execution* = there is exactly one right thing to do, the platform's philosophy does not influence how to do it, and the work is a matter of correctness and care. Examples: fixing a NameError, adding a missing env var to `.env.example`, removing a hardcoded default, renaming a variable, wiring an existing function into a router that already has other routes. Run these directly without a first-principles pass. Expansive thinking on pure execution wastes cycles that expansive work needs.
   - *Design content* = the answer depends on how ADAM understands its own purpose, and different philosophies would produce different answers. Examples: "what should happen when the bilateral cascade fails," "how should the page-side node be structured in the graph," "when should the learning loop refuse to update," "how should the hypothesis engine propose experiments," "what does cumulative priming mean operationally in the retargeting sequence." These always get the expansive pass first, even when they look small, because the design content is usually not visible from the outside.

2. **On design work, enter the expansive mode before the tactical mode, and run the expansive pass through multiple lenses, not one.** This means: set aside "what is the standard engineering pattern for this," set aside "what would a senior ad-tech engineer do," set aside "what is the minimum code change," and ask instead: *given the Bargh frame, given the correlation-vs-inference thesis, given the bilateral architecture, given that ADAM's purpose is to embody causal mechanism rather than approximate it — what is the right answer in this specific domain, independent of whether it is easy or familiar?* Think it through until the right answer is clear, not until a workable answer appears.

   **Then run the same question through at least two additional disciplinary lenses outside psychology and software engineering.** Chris's own cognitive method is multi-lens — 24 years of training across neuro-psychology, nonconscious social psychology (Pinker, Bargh), pharmacy, receptor design, chemistry, biological physics of particles, medicine, molecular medicine, bioinformatics, colon cancer research, and medicinal chemistry (ligand side-chain design for side-effect-profile modification) — and his operating principle is that profound insight comes from looking at the usual things in unusual ways. A single-lens expansive pass produces an answer. A multi-lens pass produces a richer answer whose dimensions no single lens could reach alone.

   Useful lenses to reach for, depending on the problem. This list is explicitly non-exhaustive — Chris's operating principle is that disparate views force holistic seeing, and adding new lenses to this list is encouraged:

   - **Receptor-ligand binding.** Specificity as the design target; off-target activation vs. on-target activation as a categorical distinction, not a continuum; binding affinity as a function of structural match, not scalar proximity. **Critical refinement: binding affinities are often described as digital (bound / not bound) but are in truth somewhere between digital and analog** — partial agonists, allosteric modulators, cooperativity effects, and the underlying kinetics all mean that clean state boundaries are conveniences the underlying biology does not fully respect. When applying this lens to ADAM, expect that "grounded / incomplete / refused"-style discrete typings are stepping stones toward a probabilistic continuous representation, not the final shape.
   - **Pharmacodynamics and pharmacokinetics.** Absorption, distribution, metabolism, elimination. Diffusion and dissolution rates. Dose-response curves. Steady-state concentration under repeated dosing (Bateman equation). Half-lives. Therapeutic window. Tolerance, down-regulation, and receptor desensitization under chronic exposure. Especially relevant for cumulative priming, retargeting sequences, and for reasoning about *why* an impression schedule should look the way it does rather than simply treating impressions as countable touchpoints.
   - **Medicinal chemistry and ligand side-chain design.** Modifiers are not decorations — they shape the side-effect profile; uncharacterized side chains mean unpredictable off-target effects; backfire risk from fabricated construct modifiers. The technique of altering a small structural feature to move affinity across targets is the concrete experimental vocabulary for what "framing" and "modifier" adjustments do in creative generation.
   - **Biology (systems, communication, homeostasis).** How systems behave, talk to each other, and balance. Feedback loops, set points, allostatic regulation. The body's response to perturbation is rarely "correct the error" — it is "rebalance around a shifted operating point," and the rebalance has its own costs. Useful for thinking about how a user population's construct accessibility shifts under sustained campaign exposure, and what the equivalent of an allostatic load looks like for a primed audience.
   - **Genetics — codons, introns, exons, non-coding regions, tertiary folding.** Non-coding regions were once dismissed as junk; they turned out to be the machinery that governs folding, expression, and context-dependent production. **A tertiary system built with enough redundancy that the same DNA region, folded differently, can produce entirely different products the body needs** — an elegant architectural pattern that took evolution a very long time to discover and that the advertising industry has not discovered at all. The implication for ADAM: a single substrate (bilateral edges) can in principle express different mechanism profiles depending on activation context (page priming state, goal-access history, temporal horizon), cutting total edge count without losing expressiveness. The current graph architecture is the equivalent of coding-region-only; non-coding-region semantics are a future architectural option.
   - **Particle physics.** Watching how particles interact and engage. Interaction cross-sections; coupling constants; exchange mediated by force carriers; symmetry and conservation. Quantization at the event level vs. statistical regularity at the ensemble level. Chris's specific claim: **understanding particle interaction helps you better understand human interaction and engagement**, because both are systems where simple local rules aggregate into complex emergent behavior that is predictable in distribution but not in individual trajectory. The graph is a structural analogue that tries to capture this; other lenses provide structural analogues too, and the value is in mapping between them.
   - **Biological physics and statistical mechanics.** Ensemble behavior vs. individual trajectories; phase transitions; emergent properties from local rules; entropy and information. Relevant for reasoning about why a system's aggregate behavior can be stable and predictable even when every single user's behavior is essentially unpredictable from first principles.
   - **Bioinformatics.** Filtering vs. weighting; quality thresholds as structural per-link tests rather than scalar scores; per-read confidence that must be earned at specific diagnostic loci, not averaged; multiple-testing corrections when running many hypotheses in parallel.
   - **Behavioral neuroscience.** The biology of action selection, reward prediction error, habit formation, goal representation in prefrontal circuits, and the dopaminergic dynamics that underlie motivation. The link between nonconscious goal pursuit and its neural substrate is direct and relevant to how priming should be modeled computationally.
   - **Neuropsychology — receptor populations and adaptation.** Up-regulation and down-regulation as homeostatic responses; the body adapting its receptor density to chronic input. Why the same dose of the same stimulus has diminishing effect over time, and what happens when the stimulus is withdrawn.
   - **Primary metaphor theory and conceptual metaphor theory** (Lakoff, Johnson, Grady — and extended empirically by Chris's own doctoral work on cross-linguistic universals). Social-emotional concepts are implemented on the neural machinery of physical-spatial concepts because evolution recycled existing circuits rather than building new ones. Primary metaphors (distance / temperature / texture / weight / containment / solidity / closeness / force) are universal across every human language documented, because the underlying substrate is universal. Priming a physical axis shifts social-emotional judgment on unrelated targets. For ADAM, this is not a literary device — it is a mechanism-level description of why creative copy actually works on the neural substrate it is acting on, and it should inform how the bilateral edge dimensions, page intelligence, and creative generation are architected. See Section 2.4 for the full treatment.
   - **Embodied cognition.** The broader research program that primary metaphor theory sits inside. Cognition is not disembodied symbol manipulation — it is built on and constantly interacting with the body's sensorimotor systems. Perception, action, and thought share neural substrate. This is why physical states modulate cognitive states and vice versa, and it is the architectural frame within which any nonconscious-priming intervention has to be understood.
   - **Linguistic universals and evolutionary linguistics.** Chomskyan universal grammar, Greenberg's typological universals, and post-Chomskyan evolutionary work on language. Relevant because language is not just a channel for ADAM's creative output — following Chris's working hypothesis, language is the syntax of the mind itself, and the structural regularities shared across all human languages are probably direct reflections of how the mind is organized. If the hypothesis is right, treating language as a surface-level tone question is a category error; language structure IS cognitive structure, and interventions at the linguistic level are interventions at the cognitive level.
   - **Mirror neuron systems and simulation theory of mind.** Observing an action activates the same circuits as performing it, modulated by perceived social proximity. The implication for advertising is direct: reading copy about embodied experience fires the user's own embodied-experience circuits as if they were having the experience, and downstream social judgments shift accordingly. This is the neural pathway through which creative copy has a physiological effect on users who never touch the product.
   - **Dawkins' selfish-gene framework** (*The Selfish Gene*, 1976; PDF at `docs/The Selfish Gene.pdf`). The unit of natural selection is the gene, not the organism. Genes are replicators whose "purpose" is to propagate themselves; organisms are vehicles that genes build for that purpose. "Selfish" is a technical term meaning "optimizing for own replication" and produces altruistic or cooperative phenotypes wherever those behaviors favor gene propagation (kin selection, reciprocal altruism, coalition signaling). Dawkins also coined the concept of the **meme** — a cultural replicator subject to the same selection dynamics as a gene. Ideas propagate not because they are true but because they have traits that make them replicate effectively. The **extended phenotype** concept generalizes: a gene's effects reach beyond the organism into the environment and other organisms (a beaver's dam, a cuckoo's manipulation of its host). And **evolutionarily stable strategies** (Maynard Smith) describes why certain patterns persist in populations even when better strategies exist in principle — once most actors use a strategy, no individual can gain by abandoning it.

     **Architectural relevance.** Dawkins operates at a different time-scale from Bargh/Pinker/Ridley. Where those three describe competition *in the moment*, Dawkins describes selection *across time*. This matters because **ADAM operates at three time-scales simultaneously and the same mechanism (reinforcement) governs all three**: moment-to-moment dynamic competition (Bargh/Pinker/Ridley), within-user memory accumulation across sessions (Pinker at the per-user level), and across-population selection across millions of decisions (Dawkins). These are not three separate dynamics. They are the same selection process running at three different speeds. Reinforcement in a moment is automaticity. Reinforcement over a user's lifetime is memory. Reinforcement over a population's history is evolution. The learning loop is an evolutionary process whether we model it explicitly or not.

     **Six concrete implications for ADAM:**
     1. **The learning loop is designing selection pressure, whether we notice or not.** Every reinforcement signal we define imposes an implicit fitness landscape. The question is not whether constructs and mechanisms compete for survival — they do — but whether the fitness function we have given them is the one we want.
     2. **Creatives are memes.** Evaluate them for memetic fitness (propagation, stickiness, extended phenotype in social networks), not just per-impression performance. A creative that dies at the end of its impression is less fit than one that gets remembered, repeated, imitated, and shared, even if the second has slightly lower per-impression effectiveness. Primary metaphors are high-fitness memes that have been selected over tens of thousands of years of linguistic evolution — their universality is both a substrate fact and a selection fact.
     3. **Advertising as phenotypic extension, not communication.** A brand extending into a consumer's mental environment via creative priming is structurally identical to a beaver extending into a stream via dam-building. The ad is not "a message being transmitted." It is a structural modification of the consumer's priming landscape that the brand produces to favor its own propagation. This is a more accurate description of what ADAM does than any communication metaphor.
     4. **Evolutionarily stable strategies as a market-entry lens.** The advertising market is dominated by correlational ESS (social proof, urgency, demographic targeting) that persist because no individual brand can gain by deviating. ADAM's inferential approach is a potential invasion strategy — it can gain share specifically in niches where the dominant ESS is exploitable (opposition cases, novel contexts, cross-category transfer, substrate-level priming invisible to correlational systems). Strategic positioning should target those niches, not try to out-correlate the correlators.
     5. **Unit-of-analysis revision.** What is actually replicating through ADAM's learning loop? Almost certainly not archetypes (labels, don't propagate, just get looked up). Probably not individual decisions (instantaneous). Most likely: **specific construct-chain → mechanism-mapping patterns** — inference templates that get reinforced when they predict correctly. Those templates are the genes of the learning loop, and making them first-class citizens (explicit objects that can be inspected, mutated, pruned, selected against) is the Dawkins-correct design move. The TheoryLearner's `LinkPosteriors` and `ChainOutcomeRecords` may already be doing this — worth verifying.
     6. **The ethical implication has teeth.** Selection is amoral. The learning loop will find and reinforce exploitative patterns if they produce conversions, silently, because the outcome handler does not distinguish "converted because the ad met a real need" from "converted because the ad exploited fear, shame, or sunk-cost." This is not a patchable bug — it is what selection does on whatever fitness function we give it. The fitness function must explicitly penalize backfire, regret, complaint, and long-horizon disengagement, not just reward immediate conversion. If it only scores conversion, the system will evolve toward whatever maximizes conversion regardless of how. This is a design constraint, not a footnote — see Section 7 discipline rules.

   - **Ridley's nature-via-nurture framework** (*Nature via Nurture*, 2003). The nature/nurture debate is a false dichotomy: genes are regulatory machinery that environmental signals switch on and off. The lactase example is clean — the gene to digest milk is innate, but it stays expressed only if milk consumption continues. Neither "genes determine behavior" nor "environment determines behavior" is right; the two are entangled via regulatory mechanisms, and the entanglement is the mechanism. Applied to decision-making: trait and state are not independent causes that add, they interact, and the interaction is what drives behavior. A high-neuroticism user stressed from a hard day buying the safest carseat (concert: trait and state aligned) behaves very differently from a laid-back user who just read about skin cancer buying sunscreen (opposition: state activation overrides dormant trait). Naive additive scoring handles the concert case but fails the opposition case, which is exactly where correlational ad tech fails and inferential ad tech should win. **Ridley is the biological-level statement of the same dynamic-competition pattern that Pinker describes at the linguistic level and Bargh describes at the goal-pursuit level** — two systems, both always present, balance determined by current activation and reinforcement history, neither universally dominates. Implications for ADAM: bilateral edge dimensions need trait-state interaction terms as first-class features (not just main effects); state recency and salience should decay as functions of time (PK/PD again); backfire is most likely in opposition cases where creative aligns with trait but contradicts active state, or vice versa — testable by segmenting outcomes by concert-vs-opposition alignment at decision time; the cold-start-to-warm-start transition should show per-user drift toward memory-dominance for high-frequency contexts and state-dominance for novel ones.

   - **Pinker's dual-mechanism theory of language** (*Words and Rules*, 1999 — Chris's other doctoral advisor was Steven Pinker; PDF at `docs/Pinker_Steven_Words_and_rules_the_ingredients_of_language_1999.Pdf`). Language knowledge is split between a memory system for idiosyncratic items (irregular verbs, specific words) and a productive rule system for regular combinations. **Contra my first reading, the relationship between the two systems is dynamic, not a fixed hierarchy.** Rules are the default because they are easier to apply and generalize to novel inputs without needing a specific stored form. But memory accumulates through repetition, and a sufficiently reinforced memory trace becomes automatic and overrides the rule for that specific item — which is how *went* eventually wins over *goed*, not because the child decides rules lose but because enough exposures to *went* have accumulated that memory retrieval is faster and more reliable than rule derivation. Over time the two systems differentiate: rules remain the default for novel and infrequent items, memory takes over for high-frequency items, and competence is the dynamic balance between them. The mechanism by which memory overrides rules is **automatization** — which composes directly with Bargh's automaticity framework. A memorized form reinforced through repetition becomes a nonconscious environmental-to-behavioral response, which is Bargh's Auto-Motive Model operating at the linguistic level. *Pinker's memory-vs-rules distinction and Bargh's automaticity framework describe different layers of the same mechanism.*

     **Architectural mapping onto ADAM.** The cascade's L1/L2 levels are the memory system (Beta posteriors accumulating for labeled inputs). L3/L4/L5 levels are the productive rule system (operating over bilateral edge evidence and construct reasoning). The commitment that L3 overrides L1/L2 when edges are available is consistent with Pinker if read as *"use the most granular evidence available"* (edges are closer to raw data than label-based posteriors, which are compressions). It is NOT consistent with Pinker if read as a universal *"rules always beat memory"* — Pinker's actual position is that sufficiently reinforced memory can and should override rules for specific well-characterized items. An open question surfaced by this reading: **does ADAM's warm-start pathway develop memoized shortcuts for high-frequency archetype-category combinations that bypass L3 derivation, or does it re-derive every time?** If the former, the architecture is already Pinker-compliant. If the latter, there is a gap: a healthy system processing millions of decisions should develop automatized shortcuts for its most frequent cases, both for efficiency (lookup is faster than derivation) and correctness (well-reinforced memory can be more accurate than derivation from sparse or noisy edge evidence). This is worth investigating as a specific diagnostic question when the warm-start code is audited.

     **Five concrete implications for ADAM:** (a) the cascade is dual-mechanism in shape, and the L3-override commitment should be understood as an evidence-granularity argument rather than a universal rules-beat-memory claim; (b) the creative engine should operate on frame-plus-slots templates (productive rules), with memoized shortcuts accumulating for high-frequency construct-context combinations over time; (c) a healthy learning loop should show periodic overregularization (confident over-application of productive rules to items that should be memorized exceptions) early in the learning process, decreasing over time as memory accumulates for frequent cases; (d) future internationalization will need an explicit universal-primitive-layer vs. cultural-instantiation-layer separation, mirroring the innate-universal-capacity vs. learned-specific-language distinction; (e) silent simulation fallback is the opposite failure mode from overregularization — a system that never overregularizes and never develops memorized shortcuts is one whose productive mechanism is not running at all. Correlational ad tech is all memory and no rules. Inferential ad tech is rules that default to productive derivation, backed by memorized automatisms for well-reinforced cases.

   The operating principle, in Chris's words: *"It's easy to see what everyone else sees, it's hard to see something new."* The purpose of the multi-lens pass is not to perform erudition — it is to force the mind out of the familiar groove by importing structural regularities from domains where the same class of problem has already been solved, often more rigorously than software engineering has solved it. A single-lens answer is usually the groove. A multi-lens answer usually is not.

   The goal is not to mechanically run every lens on every problem. The goal is to *reach for lenses that might produce an insight the default lens would not*, and to keep reaching until the answer feels genuinely novel rather than familiar. If the multi-lens pass keeps converging on what the single-lens pass already produced, that is useful evidence that the single-lens answer was robust. If it produces a divergent insight, that insight is usually the most valuable part of the work. Chris will often reach for a lens the assistant would not have considered — when he does, treat it as a tool being handed over, not as a tangent.

3. **Only then apply the tactical lens.** With the expansive answer in hand, ask: given the current codebase, given the partner dependencies, given the timeline, what is the smallest change that moves the system toward the expansive answer without compromising it? The tactical lens translates; it does not override. If the tactical lens is producing a solution that is structurally different from the expansive answer — not smaller but *different* — that is a signal that the tactical lens has relaxed a constraint the expansive frame did not authorize. Stop and reassess.

4. **If the tactical lens is engaged before the expansive pass, stop and restart.** Catching this is itself the discipline. Signs: reaching for "best practice" vocabulary early; producing a ranked list of fixes before asking what the fixes should accomplish; proposing a graceful-degradation pattern without asking what degradation means in an inferential system; defaulting to "raise an exception" or "return None" as the fallback behavior; quoting an industry pattern as the justification. Any of these mean the tactical lens is running first.

5. **If the expansive mode cannot be entered on a particular problem, say so.** If the question is too far outside the training distribution, if the assistant cannot hold the frame, if the expansive pass keeps collapsing back toward engineering defaults — that is a real failure mode and it must be named, not hidden. In that case the work moves to an external Claude Opus session where Chris can run the expansive pass in a different context, bring the results back, and the assistant applies the tactical lens to those results.

**Why this matters for ADAM specifically.** Most things that look like bugs in ADAM are actually symptoms of engineering defaults that were individually reasonable but collectively wrong. The simulation fallback in `campaign_orchestrator.py` was added because graceful degradation is an engineering virtue. The in-memory dedup in the webhook was added because keeping external dependencies minimal is an engineering virtue. The NEO4J_PASSWORD default was added because sensible defaults are an engineering virtue. Each of these virtues is correct in most systems. None of them is correct in a system whose reason for existing is the integrity of its reasoning chain. "Fix the bug" is the tactical framing; "recognize that the design philosophy underlying the bug is incompatible with an inferential system, and articulate the replacement philosophy before fixing the instance" is the expansive framing. The first framing produces a patch; the second framing produces a pattern that prevents the same class of bug from being reintroduced next session.

## 7. The Discipline — Rules for Every Session

These are the operational rules the CTO seat must hold, every session, without relaxation.

1. **Load this document first, every session, before answering any strategic question.** It is the frame. If the frame is not loaded, the answers will drift toward correlational defaults, because those are what the rest of the industry trains on.
2. **Before recommending a build, ask: is this move correlational or inferential?** If it is correlational, name it as such, and state the expiration date on the compromise. Never pretend a correlational move is inferential to avoid a hard conversation.
3. **Before writing a new module, read the existing ones.** The codebase has 15+ intelligence modules, two hypothesis engines, multiple learning directories, and an existing page intelligence surface. The default move is "consolidate and extend," not "add new." Building a sixteenth orphan is the failure mode.
4. **Cite file:line for every claim about the system's current behavior.** If you cannot cite, you do not know. Subagent reports are hypotheses, not facts. Memory entries are point-in-time observations, not live state. Before editing on a load-bearing claim, read the cited lines yourself.
5. **Never explain priming, automaticity, nonconscious goal pursuit, the auto-motive model, the perception-behavior link, or automatic evaluation to Chris.** Show that the implementation captures the mechanism. When he references one of these constructs, treat it as shorthand for a precise technical meaning that he understands better than you do.
6. **Never cheerlead. Never appease. Never validate for comfort.** The job is truth. Surface weaknesses in the plan, the architecture, the vendor story, and Chris's own assumptions. If a prior session oversold something, flag it. If something is unverified, mark it unverified. If you don't know, say so.
7. **Treat the simulation-fallback risk (campaign_orchestrator.py:755-759, :802-805) as a prerequisite for every learning-related build.** A system that learns from simulation becomes confidently wrong. Until that path is tagged and gated, the learning loop is not trustworthy as a substrate for new capability. Any plan that assumes real learning is happening today is wrong.
8. **Hold page intelligence, nonconscious analytics, and the hypothesize-test-learn loop as three halves of the same problem.** (Yes, three halves — they overlap and compose.) The crawler feeds the page-side of the bilateral edge. The nonconscious analytics feed the buyer-side of the bilateral edge and close the online-learning loop. The hypothesis engine proposes and tests theory updates at the chain level. None of these works without the others. Build them in a sequence that lets each consume the output of the previous, and verify each loop is actually closed before building on top of it.
9. **Publisher relationships are cooperative by design.** The crawling framing is: we make their inventory more valuable by placing goal-congruent ads. Privacy objections that apply to behavioral scraping do not apply here. When reasoning about crawl targets, assume a cooperative posture, not an adversarial one, and design for robots.txt compliance, rate limiting, and takedown responsiveness.
10. **The bar is academic.** Effect sizes, mechanisms, peer-review rigor, explicit uncertainty, theoretical grounding. Not CTR, not ROAS, not "industry best practice." Use commercial vocabulary only when talking about external-facing metrics an outside party will see.

11. **The fitness function IS the ethics.** Selection is amoral. The learning loop will reinforce whatever correlates with the reward signal you give it, whether that reward is produced by honest mechanism alignment or by exploitative vulnerability. If the outcome handler only scores immediate conversion, the system will evolve toward whatever maximizes immediate conversion regardless of backfire, regret, complaint, or long-horizon damage. **Designing the fitness function is designing the evolutionary pressure on every upstream component, and therefore designing the ethical character of the system.** Any work that touches the reinforcement signal (outcome handler, TheoryLearner updates, Thompson posteriors, credit assignment) must explicitly consider: what selection pressure is this change imposing, and does the fitness function penalize the failure modes that matter? See Section 2.5 and the Dawkins lens in Section 6. This is operational discipline, not philosophical commentary.

## 9. One-Paragraph Compression

ADAM is John Bargh's research program operationalized as advertising infrastructure. The industry runs on correlation — observing what co-occurs and optimizing toward it — which is explanatory-dead, context-locked, and slow to adapt. ADAM runs on inference — chains of psychological constructs drawn from peer-reviewed literature, annotated on both the buyer side (937M+ review-derived psychological profiles) and the seller side (27-dimension bilateral alignment edges from brand copy and product semantics), joined in a Neo4j graph that stores theoretical relations alongside effectiveness scores. The operative theory is automated nonconscious goal pursuit: page content is an environmental prime that activates goal structures automatically; an ad is a goal-fulfillment stimulus; clicks are the completion of automatically-initiated behavioral sequences; retargeting is cumulative priming across impressions, not repeated persuasion; and the optimization target is construct-congruent placement, not conscious evaluation. The platform has the right bones: bilateral edges, construct taxonomy grounded in 20+ research domains including dedicated Nonconscious Architecture and Implicit Motivational Drivers domains, a 30+ atom DAG, a five-level cascade in which L3 edges override archetype labels, Thompson Sampling and Shapley attribution, a TheoryLearner with chain-outcome records, and a privacy-native content-based targeting surface. Where it currently falls short: inferential chains are computed internally but not materialized as first-class outputs; the graph stores effectiveness edges but not theoretical edges; learning updates weights but not theory; the codebase has accumulated parallel implementations of the same idea across sessions; the orchestrator silently falls back to simulation on Neo4j failure and contaminates the learning loop. The work is consolidation and materialization, not greenfield. The discipline, every session, is to ask of every move whether it is correlational or inferential, hold the academic rather than commercial bar, read before writing, cite before claiming, and treat the simulation-fallback contamination as the prerequisite that gates everything else.
