The Core Insight

Every person viewing an ad is in a nonconscious goal state activated by the content they're currently reading. A Forbes article about leadership activates competence-verification goals. A travel article activates novelty-exploration goals. Our system identifies which goal is active and serves an ad that fulfills that goal. The viewer notices the ad automatically — not because it's flashy, but because their goal-directed attention system is already searching for exactly what the ad offers.

This is grounded in 35+ years of peer-reviewed research on nonconscious goal pursuit (Bargh, 1990; Chartrand et al., 2008; Custers & Aarts, 2010). The effect is causal, not correlational — meta-analyses show d=0.33-0.67 across 133 studies.

The Business Case
Why INFORMATIV is a fundamentally different advertising product.

What We Replace

Conventional ad targeting uses demographics and interests — age, income, browsing history. This is correlational: "people who look like past converters might convert." INFORMATIV uses psychological state — the actual nonconscious goal active in the viewer's mind at the moment of ad exposure. This is causal: "the page activated this goal, and our ad fulfills it."

The difference: demographics tell you WHO someone is. We know WHERE their mind is RIGHT NOW.

Competitive Moat

1. Bilateral edge data. 6.75 million edges encoding both buyer AND seller psychology. No one else has both sides annotated. Building this required processing 937M+ reviews through LLM annotation. This data cannot be replicated quickly.

2. Nonconscious goal activation. Grounded in 35+ years of peer-reviewed cognitive science (Bargh, Chartrand, Custers & Aarts). This isn't a black-box ML model — it's a theoretically grounded system with known causal mechanisms.

3. Active learning. Every impression makes the system smarter. The Bayesian posteriors, within-subject design, and epistemic value computation mean the system converges on optimal targeting faster than any A/B testing framework.
